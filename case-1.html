Case Time: 00:00

A 58 year old gentleman with a history of alcohol abuse and hepatitis C is brought to the Emergency Department by ambulance after being found unresponsive by family members. Upon arrival he is found to be in acute hypoxemic respiratory failure requiring emergent intubation. A chest x-ray shows a left lower lobe infiltrate. Initial vital signs are temperature 39.1 C, HR 128, BP 78/53, RR 30. The patient is started on norepinephrine. The patient’s complete initial labs are given in the labs tab. Creatinine is 2.7, up from 0.86 one month earlier. His urine output in the first 4 hours is 44 cc’s of urine. The decision is made to start the patient on continuous renal replacement therapy. A 15 cm Marhurkar in the right internal jugular vein.
